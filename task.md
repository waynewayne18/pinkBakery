Bristol-Pink is the newest bakery chain in town. It operates five bakeries/cafes located near
school academies and caters for the families of children attending the schools, as well as
nearby office workers.
Each bakery offers a range of edible produce as well as a range of on demand created coffees.
Bristol-Pink directors want to minimise food waste and want to use AI and machine learning
algorithms to predict the volume of sales at each café. This will allow them to buy the right
volume of products that they will sell per day and minimise the amount of food that will need to
be discarded at close of each café, yielding a loss.
You are required to build a standalone electronic dashboard that will display an analysis of
previous sales of key products that could yield high losses if not sold on the day. The system will
offer different views of data analysis and graphical representation. You will need to select
suitable algorithms and apply them on the data provided. The system will then produce
predictions of sales per café for the following four weeks.
Separately to the core system views described above, you will need to evaluate the accuracy of
the algorithms that you have experimented with and display the information on an optional
choice on the dashboard.
Therefore, will need to complete the following Deliverables.

Your dashboard should be able to read files of data in CSV format, to process them and display
the outcomes graphically.
• Identify the top three selling foods and the top three selling coffees and display the sales
fluctuation over a period of four weeks, in graphical format.
• Select suitable algorithms (any AI & Machine Learning) and apply them to predict the
sales of the selected products over the next four weeks.
• Display the predicted sales of each item in a separate graphical representation.
• Allow the user to focus on a selected period (a few days) to zoom and view the detail of
the predictions.
• Allow the user to change the training period and explore predictions with different
lengths of training data (between 4 to 8 weeks) and compare the results. In each case
the system should offer the option to provide tabular and graphical format of the
predicted sales.
